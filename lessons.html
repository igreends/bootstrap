Kids Lessons

RED 1 TENNIS PROGRAM for CHILDREN 4 - 6 YEARS

Kids 4 to 6 years old. 36' court. Racquet up to 21"

  • Track and move the ball
  • Build balance and coordination skill
  • Use simple strokes
  • Track and move to the ball
  • Develop stroke shape to build a rally
  • Keep score and understand some basic rules
  • Serve with simple action
  • for 21 weeks session

RED 2 TENNIS PROGRAM for CHILDREN 5 - 7 YEARS OLDS

Kids 5 to 7 years old. 36' court. Racquet up to 21" - 23"

  • Build the action to direct the serve
  • Judge ball depth and direction
  • Use basic footwork patterns
  • Refine groundstrokes shapes including grips on forehand and backhand
  • Change direction of the ball to hit to defined spaces
  • Move to the net and volley
  • Score the tiebreak and know all the rules of the game

ORANGE 1 TENNIS PROGRAM for CHILDREN 7 - 9 YEARS OLDS

Kids 7 to 9 years old. 60' court.
Racquet to 23" - 25"

  • Refine the serve to increase direction and speed
  • Track the faster moving orange ball
  • Increase movement speed, coordination and dynamic balance
  • Increase spin and speed on groundstrokes to move the opponent
  • Link shots into patterns of play and understand court geography
  • Approach and volley using sound patterns and technique
  • Identify their own levels of competence

ORANGE 2 TENNIS PROGRAM for CHILDREN 7 - 10 YEARS OLDS

Kids 7 to 10 years old. 60' court.
Orange and Green Dot balls. Racquet up to 25" - 26"

  • Use the serve as a weapon, keeping the returner back
  • Anticipate based upon an opponent's position and swing shape
  • Master dynamic balance and complex coordination
  • Take time away from an opponent by hitting earlier
  • Use patterns of play effectively
  • Use good decision-making to go to the net and execute on a variety of volley skills
  • Identify their own strengths and areas that need more practice

GREEN/YELLOW PROGRAM for CHILDREN 7 and Up

Green or Yellow program's made to work on more advanced technique and tactics for kids age 7 and up.
Using regular balls and courts

  • Use the serve as a weapon, keeping the returner back
  • Anticipate based upon an opponent's position and swing shape
  • Master dynamic balance and complex coordination
  • Take time away from an opponent by hitting earlier
  • Use patterns of play effectively
  • Use good decision-making to go to the net and execute on a variety of volley skills
  • Identify their own strengths and areas that need more practice
  • for 21 weeks session
